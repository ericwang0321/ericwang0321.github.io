给 AI「喂饭」的三家公司The Companies That Feed AI

大模型的能力来自数据，而最贵的数据来自人。Scale AI、Surge AI、Mercor 用三种完全不同的方式，把「人类智慧」加工成模型能学习的养料——这一页带你看懂它们各自的原理、价值与软肋。Model capability comes from data — and the most valuable data comes from people. Scale AI, Surge AI, and Mercor each turn human intelligence into training fuel in radically different ways. This page unpacks how each one works, where the value sits, and where the cracks are.

在 AI 产业链里，这三家公司都卡在同一个位置：模型公司的上游。它们不造芯片、不训模型，只做一件事——组织人类，生产模型学不到、算不出的那部分知识。All three companies occupy the same slot in the AI value chain: directly upstream of the model labs. 模型为什么需要买「人的数据」？四个阶段，需求逐级升高Why do labs pay for human data? Four stages, each more demanding

阶段一Stage 1预训练 = 读遍互联网Pretraining = reading the internet
模型先「读」海量网络文本学会语言。这些数据几乎免费——但读完只会「接话」，不会「干活」，而且互联网上的高质量文本已经快被用尽了。Models first "read" massive web text to learn language. That data is nearly free — but it only teaches the model to continue text, not to be useful. And the internet's supply of high-quality text is running out.
阶段二Stage 2监督微调（SFT）= 请家教做示范Supervised fine-tuning = hiring tutors to demonstrate
雇人写出「理想回答」给模型模仿：问题怎么答、代码怎么写、格式怎么排。这是数据层公司的第一桶金。Humans write model answers for the AI to imitate — how to respond, how to code, how to format. This was the data layer's first big business.
阶段三Stage 3人类反馈强化学习（RLHF）= 请评委打分RLHF = hiring judges to score
模型对同一问题生成多个回答，人类挑出更好的那个。数百万次「A 比 B 好」的选择训练出一个「奖励模型」，再用它调教主模型。ChatGPT 之所以「懂事」，靠的就是这道工序。The model generates multiple answers; humans pick the better one. Millions of "A beats B" judgments train a reward model, which then steers the main model. This step is why ChatGPT feels helpful rather than random.
阶段四 · 当下Stage 4 · Now专家数据 + RL 环境 = 请教授出题、建模拟考场Expert data + RL environments = professors writing exams, and simulated worlds to practice in
模型已经超过普通标注员的水平，只有医生、律师、资深工程师才能「教」它、给它「判卷」。同时，训练 AI 智能体（Agent）需要仿真工作环境——模拟的浏览器、代码库、报税系统——让模型在里面反复试错。这是 2025–26 年增长最快、单价最高的需求。Models now outperform average annotators, so only doctors, lawyers, and senior engineers can still teach or grade them. Training AI agents additionally requires simulated workplaces — fake browsers, codebases, tax systems — where models practice by trial and error. This is the fastest-growing, highest-priced demand of 2025–26.
一句话总结这门生意：The business in one sentence: 三家公司本质上都是「人类智慧的供应链」——下游是出钱的 AI 实验室，上游是供智慧的人，中间靠招募、质检、工具化把「人的判断」变成标准化商品。区别只在于：组织什么样的人、用什么方式组织。All three run a supply chain for human intelligence — AI labs pay on one end, people supply judgment on the other, and the company in the middle recruits, quality-controls, and packages that judgment into a standardized product. The only real difference: which humans they organize, and how.

它到底在做什么：把一堆生数据变成模型的教材What it actually does: turning raw data into teaching material

客户（车厂、AI 实验室、政府）把原始数据和需求交给 Scale，Scale 负责整个加工过程。它的核心能力不是「有很多人」，而是把标注拆成流水线的这套系统：Clients — carmakers, AI labs, governments — hand Scale raw data and requirements; Scale runs the entire refinement process. Its core asset isn't "lots of workers" — it's the assembly-line system itself:

接单：客户给原始数据Intake: client delivers raw data

例如自动驾驶公司的百万帧激光雷达点云，或 AI 实验室的十万条对话记录。Say, a million LiDAR frames from a self-driving fleet, or 100k conversation logs from an AI lab.

拆解：平台把大任务切成微任务Decompose: the platform slices work into micro-tasks

「标出这张图里所有行人」「判断这两个回答哪个更好」——每个任务几分钟能完成，可以分发给全球任何一个标注员。"Box every pedestrian in this frame," "pick the better of these two answers" — each takes minutes and can be routed to any worker worldwide.

人机协同：机器先标，人只改错 ← 成本的关键杠杆Human-in-the-loop: machines pre-label, humans only fix errors ← the key cost lever

Scale 用自己的模型先自动预标注，人类只负责检查和修正。机器做 80% 的粗活，人做 20% 的判断——这让 Scale 能比纯人力便宜得多，也是它和普通外包公司的本质区别。Scale's own models pre-label everything automatically; humans just review and correct. Machines do 80% of the grunt work, people supply the 20% of judgment — which is why Scale undercuts pure-labor outsourcers, and what separates it from a BPO shop.

质检：用统计学防止「人不靠谱」QA: statistics against human unreliability

同一任务发给多人投票取共识；题目里混入已知答案的「金标准题」暗中考核每个人；每个标注员有实时准确率评分，掉分就被移出任务池。The same task goes to multiple workers for consensus voting; "gold questions" with known answers are secretly mixed in to test everyone; each worker carries a live accuracy score and gets pulled from the pool if it drops.

交付：像调用云服务一样调用「人力」Deliver: labeled data via API, like a cloud service

客户通过 API 提交数据、收回标好的结果——把「组织几万人干活」封装成了一个接口。这就是它自称「数据引擎」的含义。Clients submit data and receive labels through an API — tens of thousands of workers wrapped behind a single interface. That's what "Data Engine" means.

深入一层：两支工人队伍 + 三条收入线Going deeper: two workforces, three revenue lines

工人从哪来？Where do the workers come from? 早期靠 Remotasks 平台在肯尼亚、菲律宾、委内瑞拉等地招募低成本众包工人，适合画框、描点这类通用任务。大模型时代又推出 Outlier 平台，按小时高薪招募程序员、写作者等高技能兼职，服务 RLHF 和专家数据需求。工人都是合同工，按件/按时计酬。Early on, the Remotasks platform recruited low-cost crowd workers in Kenya, the Philippines, and Venezuela for generalist tasks like drawing bounding boxes. For the LLM era, Scale launched Outlier — hourly, well-paid gigs for programmers and writers serving RLHF and expert-data demand. All are contractors paid per task or per hour.

钱从哪来？Where does the money come from? ① 数据业务：按标注量/项目收费，毛利 = 客户付费 − 工人工资 − 质检成本，靠预标注工具把毛利做厚；② 政府业务：Donovan 平台为美军做情报分析 AI，2026 年拿下约 $500M 五角大楼合同——需要安全资质，竞争者极少；③ 企业业务：帮 EY、思科等传统企业搭建内部 AI 应用（新 CEO 的转型重点）。① Data: fees per label or per project; gross margin = client fees minus worker pay minus QA cost, fattened by pre-labeling tools. ② Government: the Donovan platform builds intelligence-analysis AI for the US military — a ~$500M Pentagon contract in 2026, in a market where security clearances keep rivals out. ③ Enterprise: building internal AI applications for companies like EY and Cisco — the new CEO's strategic focus.

怎么收费：对客户有三类收费模式How it charges: three customer pricing models

Scale 不是只有一种统一报价。客户规模、是否需要 Scale 提供工人，以及项目属于商业数据服务还是政府/大型企业合同，决定了采用哪一种收费模式。Scale does not use one universal price. The model depends on customer size, whether Scale supplies the workforce, and whether the engagement is a commercial data service or a government / large-enterprise contract.

第一类：自助平台——按使用量付费Model 1: self-serve platform — usage-based pricing

适合小型客户、研究团队或试验性需求。客户自行上传数据、配置工作流并使用平台工具，按照实际消耗的标注单位或平台用量付费。它更接近云服务的 pay-as-you-go，而不是签一个完整的人工标注外包项目。Designed for smaller customers, research teams, or experimental workloads. Customers upload data, configure workflows, and use the platform tools, paying for labeling units or platform usage consumed. It resembles cloud pay-as-you-go rather than a fully managed labor engagement.

第二类：Scale 提供工人的全托管标注项目Model 2: fully managed annotation with Scale-provided workers

Scale 不只提供软件，还负责招募和调度标注员、质检以及最终交付。项目可以按图片、视频分钟、对话、偏好比较、专家回答、annotation task 或整批数据集计价；通常先做 pilot，再通过 MSA、SOW 或 Order Form 约定数量、单价、质量门槛和交付时间。Annotation task 就是一个标注员完成的最小工作单元，例如框出一张图里的行人，或比较两个模型回答并解释哪个更好。Scale supplies not only the software but also worker recruitment and routing, QA, and final delivery. Projects may be measured by image, video minute, conversation, preference comparison, expert answer, annotation task, or full dataset; a pilot is often followed by an MSA, SOW, or order form defining volume, price, quality thresholds, and delivery dates. An annotation task is the smallest unit completed by one annotator—for example, boxing pedestrians in one image or comparing two model answers and explaining which is better.

Pilot、MSA、SOW、Order Form 分别是什么意思？What do Pilot, MSA, SOW, and Order Form mean?

Pilot： 先用一小批任务测试 Scale 的质量、耗时和成本，再决定是否正式放量。A small trial batch used to test Scale's quality, turnaround time, and cost before scaling up.

MSA（Master Services Agreement）： 长期合作的总框架，主要规定保密、数据安全、知识产权、付款和违约责任。The master framework for the relationship, covering confidentiality, data security, IP, payment, and liability.

SOW（Statement of Work）： 某个具体项目要做什么、怎么做，以及需要达到什么质量标准。Defines what a specific project will do, how it will be performed, and the required quality standards.

Order Form： 具体采购多少、单价和总价是多少，以及开始时间、交付时间和付款安排。Records the purchased volume, unit and total price, start and delivery dates, and payment schedule.

整句话的意思：In plain language: 客户先用少量任务测试质量；测试通过后，双方签长期合作框架，并针对正式项目约定数量、价格、质量要求和交付期限。The client first tests quality with a small batch; if it passes, the parties sign a long-term framework and define the volume, price, quality requirements, and deadline for the full project.

第三类：政府和大型企业合同Model 3: government and large-enterprise contracts

这类业务通常不是简单的单条标注计费，而是多年期合同、固定项目总额、最低采购承诺或合同上限。报价还会覆盖安全资质、专属部署、数据隔离、项目管理以及持续交付等要求。These engagements are usually not billed as simple per-label transactions. They may use multi-year contracts, fixed project values, minimum purchase commitments, or contract ceilings, with pricing also covering security clearances, dedicated deployment, data isolation, program management, and ongoing delivery.

它的赚钱逻辑How the economics work

客户买的是「合格的数据或项目交付」，而不是单纯购买工时。The customer buys an accepted data or project deliverable—not labor hours alone. Scale 的毛利 = 客户支付的项目费或平台收入 − 标注员报酬 − 质检和项目运营成本。机器预标注越多、人工只需修正越少，单位交付成本就越低；政府和大型企业合同则还可能依靠长期承诺提高收入可见性。Scale's gross margin equals project or platform revenue minus worker pay, QA, and program operating costs. The more work machines pre-label and humans merely correct, the lower the unit delivery cost; government and large-enterprise contracts can also improve revenue visibility through long-term commitments.

2025 年的剧变：Meta 的 143 亿美元「半收购」The 2025 earthquake: Meta's $14.3B semi-acquisition

2025 年 6 月，Meta 以 $14.3B 买下 Scale 49% 的无投票权股份（估值 $29B），创始人 Wang 带队加入 Meta 领导「超级智能实验室」。这笔交易的本质：Meta 买的不是公司，是人和数据管道。但代价立刻显现——In June 2025, Meta paid $14.3B for a 49% non-voting stake (a $29B valuation), and founder Wang left to lead Meta's Superintelligence Lab. Meta wasn't really buying the company — it was buying the man and the data pipeline. The cost showed up immediately:

中立性破产：Neutrality, broken: 数据公司能看到客户的训练数据——那是 AI 实验室最核心的机密。Meta 入股后，OpenAI、Google 等大客户迅速削减订单：没人愿意让竞争对手的「关联公司」看自己的底牌。这一课定义了整个行业：数据层公司的第一资产是中立。（最大受益者是 Surge 和 Mercor。）A data vendor sees its clients' training data — the most sensitive secret an AI lab has. After Meta's investment, OpenAI, Google and others slashed their orders: nobody shows their cards to a competitor's affiliate. That episode defined the industry's first law: a data-layer company's most valuable asset is neutrality. (The biggest beneficiaries: Surge and Mercor.)

新 CEO Jason Droege（前 Uber 高管）的应对：收缩标注主业，转向两个不怕「中立性」问题的市场——政府（五角大楼合同翻了 5 倍）和企业应用。同时 Meta 承诺 5 年内每年至少采购 $450M 服务，给了收入一个保底。New CEO Jason Droege (ex-Uber) responded by de-emphasizing labeling and doubling down on two markets immune to the neutrality problem — government (the Pentagon contract grew 5x) and enterprise applications. Meta also committed to at least $450M/year of purchases for five years, putting a floor under revenue.

    它做什么：给最前沿的模型当「品味教练」What it does: taste coaching for frontier models

    Surge 和 Scale 卖的名义上是同一种东西（人类反馈数据），但定位完全相反。要理解 Surge 的原理，得先理解 RLHF 里「质量」为什么值钱：Surge nominally sells the same thing as Scale — human feedback — but from the opposite end of the market. To see why, you need to understand why quality is worth so much in RLHF:

    模型生成多个候选回答The model generates several candidate answers

    同一个问题，模型写出 A、B 两版（或更多）。For one prompt, the model writes versions A and B (or more).

    人类评委选出更好的，并说明理由A human judge picks the better one — with reasons

    哪个更准确？更安全？推理有没有偷懒？这一步的难度取决于问题的难度：判断两首打油诗容易，判断两段肿瘤治疗建议就需要医生。Which is more accurate? Safer? Did the reasoning cut corners? The difficulty scales with the question: ranking limericks is easy; ranking oncology advice takes a doctor.

    数百万次选择训练出「奖励模型」Millions of judgments train a reward model

    奖励模型 = 人类品味的数字化替身，之后由它 7×24 小时给主模型「判卷」，把人的偏好放大成机器规模。The reward model is a digital stand-in for human taste — it then grades the main model around the clock, amplifying human preference to machine scale.

    关键在于：垃圾进，垃圾出——而且是指数级放大The catch: garbage in, garbage out — exponentially amplified

    奖励模型会把标注里的每一个错误偏好放大百万倍。一批便宜但马虎的标注，可能让整个训练白费。这就是 Surge 存在的原理：在这道工序上，1 个顶尖评委 > 100 个平庸评委。The reward model magnifies every bad judgment a million-fold. One batch of cheap, sloppy labels can poison an entire training run. This is Surge's reason to exist: at this step, one excellent judge beats a hundred mediocre ones.

    追问：Agent 时代，Mercor 的机会比另外两家更大吗？A closer look: in the agent era, does Mercor have the biggest upside?

    一个常见判断：模型基础能力已经训到一定程度，下一战场是 AI Agent——而复杂任务恰恰最需要各领域专家，所以 Mercor 赢面最大。这个判断一半对，一半要打折：A popular thesis: base models are largely trained, the next battleground is AI agents — and complex tasks are exactly where domain experts matter most, so Mercor wins biggest. Half right, half in need of a discount:

    ✓ 成立的部分：Agent 需求确实撞上 Mercor 的强项✓ What holds: agent demand hits Mercor's sweet spot

    Agent 要学的是「完成整件工作」，这产生三类只有专家能干的活：设计任务（什么才算真实的法律尽调流程）、示范轨迹（专家亲手做一遍给模型学）、判定结果（这份产出在行业里算合格吗）。这些人不在众包平台上，而「快速成规模找到他们」正是 Mercor 的引擎。$450M→$1.5B 的年化增长就是这个逻辑的验证。Agents must learn to complete whole jobs, which creates three tasks only experts can do: designing tasks (what does real legal due diligence look like?), demonstrating trajectories (an expert does the job once for the model to learn from), and judging outcomes (would this pass in the profession?). Those people aren't on crowdsourcing platforms — and finding them fast, at scale, is exactly Mercor's engine. The $450M→$1.5B run-rate jump is this thesis playing out.

    ✗ 要打折的部分：三个反作用力✗ The discounts: three counterforces

    ① Agent 训练的另一半瓶颈是 RL 环境本身——搭模拟浏览器、代码库、ERP 系统是软件工程活，不是专家供给活（Mercor 收购 Sepal 正是在补这块；而环境和评分标准设计一体，这是 Surge 的看家领域）。② 有专家 ≠ 交付可用数据，中间隔着质检、校准、与训练管线对接——Surge 的整个体系建在这层，Mercor 的市场模式天然更松。③ 凡是结果能自动验证的任务（代码跑不跑得通），恰恰不需要人判卷；专家只在难验证领域（医疗、法律、开放式研究）不可替代——这个域比想象的窄。① The other half of the agent bottleneck is the RL environment itself — building simulated browsers, codebases, and ERP systems is software engineering, not expert supply (the Sepal acquisition patches exactly this; and environment design is inseparable from rubric design, Surge's home turf). ② Having experts ≠ delivering usable data — QA, calibration, and pipeline integration sit in between, the layer Surge's whole system is built on, where a marketplace is inherently looser. ③ Any task whose outcome can be verified automatically (does the code pass?) needs no human grader; experts stay irreplaceable only where verification is hard — medicine, law, open-ended research — a narrower domain than it looks.

    结论：Verdict: Agent 时代的最大增量流向「专家 × 环境」的组合。Mercor 从专家侧往上爬、正在买入环境能力；Surge 从质量/评估侧往上爬、正在扩专家池——两家从两头爬同一座山，Mercor 斜率更陡，但谈不上「机会明显更多」。真正在这个战场掉队的是 Scale：众包基因两头都不占优，所以才转向政府与企业应用。The agent era's biggest prize goes to whoever combines experts × environments. Mercor is climbing from the expert side and buying environment capability; Surge is climbing from the quality/evaluation side and deepening its expert pool — two routes up the same mountain. Mercor's slope is steeper, but not decisively so. The one actually falling behind on this battlefield is Scale: its crowdsourcing DNA wins on neither front — which is precisely why it pivoted to government and enterprise.

    行业未来：这门生意能做多久？The big question: how long does this business last?

    数据层的宿命问题：它靠「模型还不如人」赚钱，而客户的目标正是「让模型超过人」。四个趋势决定三家公司的命运——The data layer's existential riddle: it profits from models being worse than humans, while its customers' explicit goal is to make models better than humans. Four forces will decide how this plays out —

    ① 需求在升级，不是在消失① Demand is upgrading, not vanishing

    低端标注（画框、简单判断）正被合成数据和 AI 评委吞噬；但专家数据、模型评估、RL 环境的单价和总量都在暴涨。市场的形状从「金字塔」变成「尖塔」：量变少，价变高。受益排序：Mercor/Surge ＞ Scale。Low-end labeling (boxes, simple judgments) is being swallowed by synthetic data and AI judges; but expert data, evaluation, and RL environments are exploding in both price and volume. The market is going from pyramid to spire — fewer units, far higher value. Beneficiary order: Mercor and Surge over Scale.

    ② RL 环境是新战场② RL environments are the new battleground

    训练能「干活」的智能体，需要成千上万个仿真工作场景（模拟的浏览器、代码库、财务系统）供模型试错。据报道 Anthropic 曾讨论 2026 年在此投入超 $10 亿。这类业务更像软件工程而非标注——重塑了三家的能力要求，也吸引了新一批创业者入场。Training agents that can do real jobs requires thousands of simulated workplaces — browsers, codebases, finance systems — for trial and error. Anthropic reportedly discussed spending over $1B on these in 2026. The work looks more like software engineering than annotation — resetting which capabilities matter and inviting a wave of new entrants.

    ③ 中立是行业的隐形宪法③ Neutrality is the industry's invisible constitution

    Scale-Meta 事件证明：谁被单一实验室「绑定」，谁就失去其他所有实验室。这条规则保护独立的 Surge 和 Mercor，也解释了为什么实验室宁愿同时养多家供应商——它们需要一个中立的数据层存在。The Scale–Meta episode proved it: bind yourself to one lab and you lose all the others. This rule shields the independent Surge and Mercor — and explains why labs deliberately keep multiple vendors alive. They need a neutral data layer to exist.

    ④ 终局考验：模型超过专家之日④ Endgame: the day models pass the experts

    如果模型在多数领域超过人类专家，「教模型」的需求会塌缩。三家的备胎各不相同：Scale 押政府和企业应用，Surge 押「独立审计」（越是超人的 AI 越需要第三方验证），Mercor 押通用劳动市场。看懂三家的分化，本质是看它们各自为这一天买了什么保险。If models surpass human experts across most domains, demand for teaching them collapses. Each firm holds a different hedge: Scale bets on government and enterprise apps; Surge on independent auditing (superhuman AI needs third-party verification more, not less); Mercor on the general labor market. Understanding how the three diverge is really about seeing what insurance each bought against that day.

    带走三句话：Three takeaways:
    1）三家做的是同一门生意——把人类智慧包装成模型的养料——但分别押注了流程（Scale）、品质（Surge）、匹配（Mercor）三种壁垒。
    2）行业的价值中枢正从「便宜的手」转向「昂贵的脑」，再转向「仿真的世界」。
    3）它们共同的风险不是彼此，而是客户：实验室既是买家，也是潜在的替代者（自建团队），甚至终结者（做出不再需要人教的模型）。     1) All three run the same business — packaging human intelligence as model fuel — but each bet on a different moat: process (Scale), quality (Surge), matching (Mercor).
    2) The industry's center of value is shifting from cheap hands to expensive minds to simulated worlds.
    3) Their common risk isn't each other — it's the customer: the labs are at once buyers, potential substitutes (in-housing), and potential terminators (building models that no longer need teachers).

    数据实务：怎么整理、怎么交付、怎么保密Data in practice: how it's processed, delivered, and protected

    前面讲的是商业模式，这一部分下沉到操作层：几万名分散在全球的人产出的数据，是怎么被整理成能用的训练集、以什么形式交到客户手里、又怎么防止互为对手的客户看到彼此的底牌。The sections above covered business models; this one goes down to the operating layer: how output from tens of thousands of distributed workers gets refined into usable training sets, in what form it reaches clients, and how vendors keep fiercely competing clients from seeing each other's cards.

    整理流程：所有人都遵循同一条流水线The pipeline: everyone runs the same assembly line

    不管是众包画框还是专家判卷，大流程是一样的：客户需求 → 写详细的标注指南（rubric，定义什么算好答案、按什么维度打分）→ 把大项目拆成小任务分发 → 标注 → 质检 → 汇总交付。真正的功夫全在质检这一环，通用武器有四件：Whether it's crowd workers drawing boxes or experts grading answers, the flow is identical: client brief → detailed annotation guidelines (a rubric defining what counts as good and how to score) → decompose the project into micro-tasks → annotate → QA → aggregate and deliver. The real craft is in QA, which relies on four universal weapons:

    ① 金标准题（Gold Tasks）① Gold tasks

    把已知正确答案的题暗中混进任务流，标注员不知道哪题是测试。答错率一高，权重下降甚至被移出任务池——这是识别「不靠谱的人」的主探测器。Questions with known answers are secretly mixed into the task stream; workers can't tell which ones are tests. A high miss rate means down-weighting or removal from the pool — the primary detector of unreliable workers.

    ② 多人共识（Consensus）② Consensus

    同一条数据发给多人独立标注，用多数投票或加权投票（资深者权重更高）定最终答案。主观性越强的任务（偏好排序、安全判断）越依赖这一招。The same item goes to multiple annotators independently; majority or weighted voting (senior judges count more) decides the final label. The more subjective the task — preference ranking, safety calls — the more this matters.

    ③ 分层审核③ Layered review

    金字塔结构：一线标注员打底 → 资深审核员复查纠错、补漏 → 交付前抽样终审。一批数据在交付前通常被过手好几遍。A pyramid: frontline annotators produce, senior reviewers correct and fill gaps, and a final sampled audit runs before delivery. A batch typically passes through several hands before it ships.

    ④ 统计监控④ Statistical monitoring

    实时看板追踪每个人的准确率、标注员间一致性（inter-annotator agreement）、耗时异常。低质量批次自动打回重做，信任分掉线的人自动换掉。Live dashboards track per-worker accuracy, inter-annotator agreement, and timing anomalies. Low-quality batches are auto-reassigned; workers whose trust score drops are automatically rotated out.

    同一条流水线，三种侧重Same pipeline, three emphases

    Scale

    重软件流水线：任务在平台里自动拆解、路由、分派；机器先预标注，人只改错；可选「标准管线」（一轮审核）或「共识管线」（多人多轮裁决），主观任务走后者。Heavy on the software pipeline: tasks are auto-decomposed, routed, and dispatched; machines pre-label and humans only fix errors; clients choose a standard pipeline (one review pass) or a consensus pipeline (multiple judges, multiple rounds) for subjective work.

    Surge

    重入口筛选 + 实时监控：先用领域资格考试和背景审查把人筛掉一大半，进来后持续用金标准题和信任评分动态考核。因为人的水平高，审核层级反而可以更薄。Heavy on entry screening plus live monitoring: domain exams and background checks filter out most applicants up front; those admitted are continuously tested with gold tasks and trust scores. Because the people are better, the review pyramid can be thinner.

    Mercor

    重匹配、轻管理：用 AI 面试给 3 万+ 专家建档、按需求精准匹配；数据生产环节靠高时薪激励质量，具体任务流程往往由客户实验室自己定，Mercor 管工时、结算和绩效追踪。Heavy on matching, light on management: AI interviews profile 30k+ experts for precise matching; quality is incentivized through premium hourly pay, task workflows are usually defined by the client lab itself, and Mercor handles hours, payouts, and performance tracking.
